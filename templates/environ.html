Positive Impacts

1. Large scale systems of microalgae are able to absorb large amounts of carbon dioxide from industrial processes, turning wasteful byproducts into a resource, helping keep its production carbon neutral and potentially mitigate climate change effects.
2. Microalgae can grow in wastewater, absorbing nutrients like nitrogen and phosphorus. This helps reduce water pollution and lower the demand for freshwater resources in cultivation.
3. Biofuels are biodegradable which reduces environmental impact incase of leakage.

Negative Impacts

1. The production of biofuel require large amounts of water which if improperly managed, could result in straining local supply and environment
2. Improperly managed cultivation can lead to unintended algae blooms, causing oxygen depletion and harming aquatic life in natural water bodies.
3. The construction and maintenance of photobioreactors or open ponds require significant materials and energy inputs, which could create additional environmental pressure
