About Us

We are a team of curious and dedicated IBDP students working together on our Group 4 Project to explore the potential of algae as a sustainable biofuel and investigate how it can address some of the pressing environmental challenges of today. Through this initiative, we strive to not only contribute to a deeper understanding of sustainable energy alternatives but also inspire others to embrace innovative approaches to creating a greener and more sustainable future.

What is the problem with fossil fuels?

Climate Change: Releases greenhouse gases, causing global warming.
Air Pollution: Releases pollutants such as sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides, leading to health issues like respiratory diseases and environmental harm.
Non-Renewable: Fossil fuels take millions of years to form and are depleting, making their continued use unsustainable.
Environmental Damage: Extraction and transportation destroy ecosystems, pollute water sources, and lead to disasters like oil spills.
Inequality: The reliance on fossil fuels creates economic and political imbalances, as resources are controlled by a few countries or corporations
